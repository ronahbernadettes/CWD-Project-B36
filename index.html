ONE DIRECTION


Introduction

One Direction, British-Irish male vocal group whose stylish good looks and bright pop-rock sound helped make it one of the best-selling boy bands of all time. One Direction released five hit albums, including Up All Night (2011) and Midnight Memories (2013), before going on an indefinite hiatus in 2016.

    • Formation

    Aspiring vocalists competed in the British television program The X Factor, where the group was created in 2010. Early in the season, the five members were encouraged to combine their talents after they each tried out and competed as solo artists. Under the moniker One Direction, the boys, who were all 16 to 18 years old at the time, garnered a lot of attention due to their captivating vocals and stage presence, particularly among young female fans. Simon Cowell, the show's creator, offered One Direction a recording contract shortly after the season concluded, despite the fact that the group ultimately failed to win on The X Factor.

    Stardom in the U.K. and Europe, and Beyond

    One Direction was marketed as a group of teenage idols who were not only musically compatible but also unique in their personalities and styles, following in the footsteps of previous pop-music boy bands. The trio entered the recording studio in early 2011 after expanding its fan base through a British Isles tour with other X Factor contestants. With its upbeat pop songs about careless celebration, first-time heartbreak, and other adolescent issues, the resulting album, Up All Night (2011), became an instant hit both domestically and abroad in other European and Commonwealth nations. The song "What Makes You Beautiful," a bouncy empowerment hymn that topped the British and Irish singles charts and won best British single at the Brit Awards (the British equivalent of the Grammy Awards) in early 2012, contributed to its rise to fame.

    One Direction, with the help of a social media effort that exposed the group to potential global fans, had little trouble breaking through, despite the fact that the American market had frequently plagued otherwise successful British boy bands. After its 2012 release, Up All Night sold over a million copies in the US and debuted at the top of the Billboard album chart. With their second album, Take Me Home (2012), the quintet continued its global success while mostly adhering to the successful formula of its debut. In 2013, the group released their third successful album, Midnight Memories, and was included in the 3-D concert-tour documentary One Direction: This Is Us
